# Initial Management of Suspected Fractures

## Primary survey first

For any significant trauma, follow ATLS principles (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure) before focusing on the limb. A dramatic-looking fracture must not distract from a life-threatening injury.

## The standard early approach: "RICE + immobilize"

- **Rest**: avoid weight-bearing or loading the injured part.
- **Ice**: short applications (15–20 minutes) with a cloth barrier to reduce swelling. Not directly on skin.
- **Compression**: soft wrap where appropriate; avoid circumferential tight wraps that risk compartment syndrome.
- **Elevation**: above the level of the heart when practical.
- **Immobilization**: splint in position of comfort; do not forcibly reduce a deformity in the field.

## Analgesia

Match analgesia to pain severity and site. Paracetamol and NSAIDs are first-line for mild-to-moderate pain in adults without contraindications. Opioids may be needed for severe pain, open fractures, or reductions. Always check allergies and renal/hepatic function before prescribing.

## Red flags requiring urgent escalation

- Open fracture (any skin breach)
- Neurovascular compromise (loss of pulse, pallor, paresthesia, paralysis, pain on passive stretch — classic "6 Ps" suggest compartment syndrome or ischemia)
- Dislocation, especially with suspected vascular injury (e.g., posterior knee dislocation)
- Pediatric spiral fracture of humerus or femur without a plausible mechanism
- Suspected pathological fracture (minimal trauma, elderly, known malignancy)
- Suspected cervical spine injury with any neurological signs

## Imaging

Two orthogonal views (usually AP and lateral) at minimum. Include the joint above and below a long-bone fracture. Consider CT for complex intra-articular or pelvic injuries. MRI is useful for occult fractures (e.g., scaphoid, hip) not visible on X-ray.

## Documentation

Always document neurovascular status **before and after** splinting or any manipulation. This is both clinically essential and medicolegally important.
